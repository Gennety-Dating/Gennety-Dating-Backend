# Gennety — Cookie Policy

**Last Updated: 23 July 2026**

**Version: 1.0** — first source-of-truth draft. The cookie-consent mechanism it
describes is **live on `gennety.com`** (banner, preferences dialog, and an
append-only consent record). This draft brings the published legal text in line
with that mechanism.

This Cookie Policy explains how Gennety ("**Gennety**", "**we**", "**us**", or
"**our**") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website `gennety.com`,
in our Telegram Mini Apps, and in our mobile application. Read it together with
our [Privacy Policy](https://gennety.com/privacy).

---

## 1. What Cookies and Similar Technologies Are

"Cookies" are small text files a website stores on your device. We also use
similar technologies:

- **Local storage** — a browser store that keeps small values (like your
  language or a consent record) until they are cleared.
- **Session identifiers** — a random value we keep in your browser so that a
  consent choice can be tied to the moment you made it, without identifying you.
- **App/Mini App local storage** — the equivalent on-device store inside
  Telegram Mini Apps and the mobile app.

Where this Policy says "cookies", it means all of these unless stated otherwise.

---

## 2. Our Approach in One Line

The website works with **necessary** storage only. Everything else —
**functional**, **analytics**, and **marketing** — is **off by default** and
runs only if you turn it on. Inside the Telegram bot we operate a
**no-third-party-tracking** model.

---

## 3. Cookie Categories

When you first visit `gennety.com`, a banner lets you **Accept all**, **Reject
non-essential**, or **Customize**. You can reopen these choices at any time via
**Cookie Preferences** in the website footer.

| Category | Can you turn it off? | What it does | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Necessary** | No — required for the site to function | Remembers your consent choice and a random session id, keeps the site secure, and stores basic UI settings such as your chosen language and theme. | Consent record and session id in local storage; language/theme preference. |
| **Functional** | Yes (off by default) | Enables optional enhancements you ask for. | The embedded **Spotify** player, which loads **only after** you enable functional cookies. Once loaded, Spotify may set its own cookies and process data under Spotify's privacy policy. |
| **Analytics** | Yes (off by default) | Helps us understand, in aggregate, how visitors use the site so we can improve it. | Currently none are active. If we add an analytics tool, it will run only with your consent and will be listed here. |
| **Marketing** | Yes (off by default) | Would be used to measure or show relevant campaigns. | Currently none are active. If we add any, they will run only with your consent and be listed here. |

Necessary storage relies on our **legitimate interest** in providing a working,
secure website (and, for the consent record itself, our **legal obligation** to
keep proof of consent). Every other category relies on your **consent**.

---

## 4. The Consent Record We Keep

Because consent law requires us to be able to demonstrate a valid consent, when
you make a choice we store an **append-only** record. It contains:

- the **action** you took (accepted / rejected / partial / withdrawn);
- which **categories** you allowed;
- the **version** of this Cookie Policy in force at the time;
- a **random session identifier** from your browser's local storage;
- the **page URL** where you made the choice;
- your browser's **user-agent** string;
- a **salted hash of your IP address** — we do **not** store the raw IP.

This record cannot be edited or deleted after it is written (it is a compliance
log), and it is not used to profile you or to advertise to you. Retention and
your rights over it are covered in the [Privacy Policy](https://gennety.com/privacy).

---

## 5. Changing or Withdrawing Your Choice

- **On the website:** open **Cookie Preferences** in the footer at any time to
  change categories or withdraw consent. Withdrawing turns every optional
  category off going forward.
- **In your browser:** you can also block or delete cookies in your browser
  settings. Blocking necessary storage may stop parts of the site working.
- **Third-party cookies** set by an embed you enabled (such as Spotify) are
  controlled by that provider; you may need to clear them through your browser
  or the provider's own controls.

When we materially change this Policy we update the version, which prompts the
banner to ask for your choice again where the law requires.

---

## 6. Telegram Mini Apps and the Mobile App

Our Telegram Mini Apps and mobile app do **not** use advertising or analytics
cookies. They use on-device local storage only for what they need to work — for
example remembering your language and theme, or holding an unsent form draft.
Everything you do inside the Telegram bot is also processed by **Telegram** under
Telegram's own policies, which we do not control.

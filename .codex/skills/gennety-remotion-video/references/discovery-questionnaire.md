# Adaptive per-video questionnaire

Use this as a decision tree, not a form to dump on the user. Extract existing answers first, then ask only what remains important. Ask in the user's language and in batches of at most three questions.

## Never re-ask by default

Do not ask for the product category, base audience, core palette, overall brand direction, typography character, primary conversion goal for acquisition, or general people aesthetic. These are fixed in `brand-profile.md`.

Ask about one of these only when the user wants a different campaign, audience segment, visual experiment, or product surface.

## Round 1: campaign job

Determine:

1. Where will the video appear: paid social, organic social, Telegram ad/post, website hero, product onboarding, app store, event, presentation, or another placement?
2. What single action should the viewer take immediately after watching?
3. What outcome matters: click-through, completed registration, awareness, explanation, retention, or announcement?

If the user says only “Сделай видео с Remotion”, recommend starting with a short acquisition video that drives registration, but ask for the placement because it changes safe areas and CTA treatment.

## Round 2: deliverables

Determine:

1. Required aspect ratios and resolutions.
2. Target duration and hard maximum.
3. Required languages and whether copy-identical or independently paced localizations are needed.
4. Frame rate or platform constraint when nonstandard; otherwise default to 30 fps.
5. Final output: MP4/H.264 by default, plus optional still, thumbnail, GIF, ProRes master, alpha, or source package.

Common recommendations:

- Reels/TikTok/Stories: 1080×1920, 9:16, keep essential content out of UI overlays.
- Website/Telegram: 1920×1080, 16:9, unless the actual embed requires another ratio.
- Short acquisition concept: 15–30 seconds. Choose the shortest duration that still makes a new product flow understandable.

## Round 3: story and proof

Determine:

1. Which product idea must dominate this video?
2. Which two or three product proofs should appear?
3. Should the story be emotional/FOMO-first, product-demo-first, testimonial-first, or a deliberate hybrid?
4. What exact CTA and destination should the final frame communicate?
5. Are there claims, dates, prices, features, or metrics that require verification?

Recommend a hybrid for Gennety acquisition: emotional hook, concise product mechanics, romantic payoff, registration CTA.

## Round 4: assets and product surfaces

Determine:

1. Which logos, photos, videos, screenshots, UI code, date cards, venue images, and copy are approved?
2. Are people real users, models, licensed stock, or generated? Are ad usage rights explicit?
3. Should the product interaction be recreated from production source, captured as a screen recording, or shown as stylized but truthful UI?
4. Which assets must not be committed or modified?
5. What is missing, and may Codex source or generate it?

Default to production-derived React UI and user-supplied approved portraits. Never browse for people or generate them merely to fill a gap.

## Round 5: audio and accessibility

Determine:

1. Music only, music plus SFX, voiceover, or silent-first?
2. If voiceover: speaker character, pace, language, and approved TTS/provider or human recording.
3. Are burned-in captions required? Must the ad make full sense muted?
4. Are music and SFX licensed for paid advertising and all target territories/platforms?

Default recommendation: make the visual cut understandable muted, then add a modern instrumental bed and restrained UI/transition SFX after visual lock. Do not add a voiceover unless it improves comprehension within the timing budget.

## Round 6: creative boundaries and approval

Determine:

1. Any current launch films, moodboards, or references to emulate at the level of rhythm, hierarchy, and finish?
2. What must be avoided beyond the fixed brand profile?
3. How much experimental freedom is allowed for this video?
4. What should be approved first: script, styleframes, silent animatic, sound direction, or final render?
5. Is there a deadline or platform review constraint?

## Completion test

The brief is ready when all statements below are true:

- One audience action is clear.
- Placement, aspect ratios, duration, language, and outputs are known.
- The hook, message order, proof, and CTA are coherent.
- Required assets and rights are known or explicitly marked as missing.
- Audio scope is decided or intentionally deferred until visual lock.
- Any product claim can be verified against current product sources.
- The user understands the proposed creative direction and any material assumption.

Then produce the creative contract and implementation plan. Do not ask low-impact taste questions that can be solved through the fixed profile and professional judgment.

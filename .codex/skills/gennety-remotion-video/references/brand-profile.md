# Gennety Dating fixed video profile

Use this profile by default. Do not re-ask these fields for each video. Treat an explicit user request as an override for that video, not as a permanent brand change unless the user says so.

## Product and promise

- Category: AI-first romantic matchmaking and date concierge.
- Core promise: move young people from discovery to a real in-person date without endless swiping, profile browsing, or user-to-user in-app chat.
- Product model: Gennety learns meaningful context, proposes one carefully chosen match, helps both people find a shared time, selects a suitable public venue, creates the date details, sends reminders, and supports the date flow.
- Primary framing: `personal AI matchmaker`, not another dating catalog.
- Desired conversion for acquisition video: registration.
- Desired feeling: curiosity, credible novelty, romantic possibility, and FOMO about time wasted in old dating apps.

Validate exact mechanics against `PRODUCT_SPEC.md` and current code before writing copy. Do not claim guaranteed relationships, a perfect match, round-the-clock human concierge service, universal venue verification, or quantified success unless the current product source supports the exact claim.

## Audience

- Core age: 18–30.
- Context: young adults in large cities.
- Primary creative lens: women-first, while remaining inclusive of men and mixed audiences.
- Social signal: modern, attractive, stylish, culturally current, and approachable.
- Avoid stereotypes, objectification, class-coded insults, or suggesting that one subculture or appearance is inherently inferior.

## Message hierarchy

Prefer this order for short acquisition videos:

1. Real dates, not endless app activity.
2. A personal AI matchmaker works autonomously and saves time.
3. Matching is based on context, values, preferences, and vibe, not only photos.
4. The product makes the new flow understandable: match, shared time, public venue, date card, support.
5. Registration is fast and simple, only when this remains true in the current product.

Use safety as reassurance, not fear-based advertising. Public, busy venues and support can be shown when verified by the product source. Coffee may appear as an easy, low-pressure example, not as a mandatory product promise.

## Visual direction

- Contemporary startup launch film with the precision of current AI-product advertising.
- Premium dating energy without old-world luxury signals.
- Young, bold, bright, clean, and conversion-oriented.
- Large, heavy, highly readable typography.
- Rounded, borderless surfaces; restrained liquid-glass and blur where they improve hierarchy.
- Realistic UI interaction: taps/clicks, selection states, zooms, focus shifts, and smooth scene transitions.
- Dense polish, low cognitive load: one primary idea per beat.
- The product should feel new and desirable, not abstract or sci-fi for its own sake.

Avoid ornate scripts, gold filigree, dated luxury motifs, generic hearts everywhere, fake 3D phone spins, chaotic particle overload, weak gray-on-gray text, overlong UI demos, and decoration that hides the product flow.

- Use heavy headline weights and controlled line length.
- Prefer the fonts already licensed and stored in `apps/video/public/fonts`.
- Keep Ukrainian glyph coverage and actual rendered line breaks under review.
- Do not use decorative calligraphy as a main type voice.

## People and media

- Prefer specific user-supplied, approved images of stylish, attractive, relatable young adults.

- Never source or generate replacement faces without explicit permission.

## Motion character

- Smooth, deliberate, responsive, and frame-accurate.
- Use strong reveals, confident easing, controlled depth, and subtle tactile UI feedback.
- Use glass, bloom, grain, or light leaks sparingly.
- Open with a legible hook quickly, preserve reading time, and give the CTA a clean final hold.
- Design 9:16 and 16:9 layouts independently while sharing data, tokens, and scene logic.
